Kabsa

Kabsa is a traditional Arabian rice dish that originates from Saudi Arabia and is popular across the Middle East. It is made with fragrant basmati rice, tender meat—often chicken, lamb, or beef—and a blend of aromatic spices such as cardamom, cinnamon, cloves, bay leaves, black lime, and saffron. The dish is cooked by simmering the meat with tomatoes, onions, and spices, allowing the flavors to infuse deeply. The rice is then cooked in the same broth, absorbing the rich, spiced essence. Kabsa is typically garnished with roasted nuts like almonds, cashews, and pine nuts,, as well as raisins for a touch of sweetness. Served on a large communal platter, it symbolizes hospitality and unity and is often accompanied by a side of spicy tomato sauce (shattah), yogurt, and fresh salad. This flavorful dish is a staple for festive occasions, family gatherings, and celebrations, offering a perfect balance of savory, spicy, and mildly sweet flavors.
